Project Handover Form — User Guide

What is this app?

The Project Handover Form is a web application built for the Core Operations Engineering team to record and manage interface handover metadata in a single, centralised location.

Previously, this information was maintained in a shared Excel spreadsheet on SharePoint. While it served its purpose, the Excel approach came with several problems:

  • No data quality control — anyone could enter data in any format, leading to inconsistent entries, typos, and missing fields
  • No audit trail — there was no way to know who changed what and when
  • Duplicate entries — without validation, the same interface could be recorded multiple times with slight variations
  • Version conflicts — multiple people editing the same file at the same time caused overwrites and lost work
  • No search or filter — finding a specific entry meant scrolling through hundreds of rows

This app solves all of those problems. It enforces clean data entry through built-in validation rules, prevents duplicates, tracks every change with a full audit history, and gives you instant search and filtering across all records.

Who is this for?

This app is for engineers and team members responsible for handing over data interfaces. If you are submitting a new interface, updating an existing one, or looking up details of a past handover — this is where you do it.

How to access the app

The app is hosted on Streamlit and uses your company login for authentication. Your email address is automatically detected when you log in — you do not need to enter it manually.

Four cards below the filters show Total Records, Source Systems, Databases, and Projects at a glance.

Export

  • Export Current View — downloads a CSV of the filtered entries you are currently viewing
  • Export All Records — downloads a CSV of every active entry in the system

Viewing entries

Each entry appears as a collapsible card showing Source System | Interface Name | Database Name | Priority. Click to expand and see all details.

Editing an entry

  1. Expand the entry and click Edit
  2. A dialog opens with all fields pre-filled
  3. Make your changes and click Update, or click Cancel to discard

The same validation rules apply. The Engineer Email field is locked and cannot be changed.

Deleting an entry

Deleting requires 3 clicks to prevent accidents:

  1. Click Delete
  2. Button changes to Confirm? — click again
  3. Button changes to DELETE! (red) — click one final time

Click Cancel at any step to abort. Deleted entries are not permanently removed — they are marked as inactive for audit purposes.

Pagination

Entries are shown 20 per page. Use the First, Previous, Next, and Last buttons at the bottom to navigate.

Tips for clean data entry

  1. Be consistent with naming — key fields are auto-uppercased, but follow your team's agreed conventions
  2. Use full Confluence URLs — copy the full URL from your browser, not a shortened link
  3. One interface per entry — do not combine multiple interfaces into one record
  4. Use commas for multiple values — e.g., multiple source contacts separated by commas
  5. Check for existing entries first — search in Tab 2 before creating a new entry

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I made a mistake in my entry. Can I fix it?
Yes. Go to Existing Entries, find your entry, click Edit, and update the fields.

Q: I accidentally deleted an entry. Is it gone forever?
No. Deleted entries are soft-deleted. Contact the Core Ops Engineering team to restore it.

Q: Why can I not change the Engineer Email field?
It is automatically set to your login email for accurate tracking.

Q: Why is the Expiration Date field hidden?
It only appears when Encrypted is set to YES.

Q: I am getting a “primary key already exists” error.
An entry with the same Source System, Interface Name, Database Name, and PSG Table Name already exists. Search for it in Tab 2 and edit it instead.

Q: Who do I contact if I have issues?
